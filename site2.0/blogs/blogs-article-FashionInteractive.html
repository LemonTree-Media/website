How Interactivity Transforms the Fashion Shopping Experience

Introduction

It is no surprise that when you think fashion, you think high-fashion and those immersed in it – Gucci socks and the rappers who flaunt them, Chanel bags and the models who stunt them, fashion shows and the celebrities who attend them. Fashion is defined by looking good and looking good is defined by what you wear and how you wear it. Like the runway is to the high-fashion experience, interactivity in the shopping experience can make or break a fashion label.

The market landscape has become virtual, and an interactive shopping experience has become the new runway.

-Kelley Liu

Although the majority of consumers are familiar with high-fashion brands, not many are frequent buyers, often because of their hefty price tag. According to Business of Fashion, “fast-fashion” – cheap and chic clothing often sold in online boutiques such as Fashion Nova and ASOS – is where the bulk of consumer attention is fixated, with share prices at all-time highs during quarantine. However, not only do fast-fashion boutiques draw inspiration from high-fashion developments, they have begun to attract consumers with interactive content. The runway, a staple of haute couture, has become something able to be experienced on e-commerce websites.

Especially during a global pandemic, fashion brands must re-evaluate their industry’s needs. These needs are increasingly digital: the most demanded jobs in fashion currently (CGI Artists, Virtual Showroom Designers, Data Editors) all relate to e-commerce, so the market landscape has become virtual, and an interactive shopping experience has become the new runway. Runways all have an element of interactivity (the most iconic having audience inclusion such as pouring rain or robots that painted the runway), an experience and space designed for high-couture culture. Now that things have moved online and away from in-person gatherings, the consumer experience in shopping for fast-fashion is what will determine success. Moreover, digital fashion shows have been unsuccessful during lockdown, reporting sharp dives in engagement levels and sales. Fashion brands must do more than just put their seasonal shows online; they have to utilize modern advances in video technology to include the buyer in the process.

Consumer experience can be transformed through interactive content in many ways. Shoppable videos are becoming increasingly popular, with users able to click on a video and “save” the outfits in the video to a look-book to be bought later. Not only is this an example of a virtual runway of sorts, but users can see what is on their favorite model and what is in stock with the click of a button. Burberry, for example, took advantage of modern technology’s access to immediate convenience – they offered a “see-now, buy-now” policy, where buyers could contact customer service directly from their shoppable video to pre-order a clickable outfit. Global retailer Alibaba also hosts an lucrative “see-now, buy-now” shopping festival where retail is transformed into entertainment via interactivity. Retailers have also begun to interact directly with their buyers through Twitter Q&As, polls, and even podcasts, making the cloistered high-society culture surrounding high-fashion more inclusive.

Interactivity is the future of the fashion industry as lockdown has caused a need for growth in digital avenues. With roots in the runway and the experience it generates for its cult followers and paparazzi, fashion is poised to change to its new home online and with access to a new userbase in apps such as Gojek. Things might not return back to “normal”, so fashion brands should expect e-commerce to be an even bigger part of the industry. Customer experience in the important time period between adding a product to cart and checking out can be transformed through interactive content, as interactivity includes the consumer into the creative process, increasing engagement with the experience. Ultimately, fashion is shifting towards the virtual, but still expects to have the human at its center, the user at its focus.
