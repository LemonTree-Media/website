How Interactivity Can Improve Streaming Experience

Introduction

With 3.8 million unique broadcasters and 1.44 million concurrent viewers on average, Twitch boasts an annual revenue of about 1.5 billion with 300 million coming from advertising sales alone. Twitch is now outstripping even traditional news outlets such as MSNBC and CNN in terms of concurrent viewership, matching FOX News and ESPN with around 1.5 million peak viewers on average. Given the current stay-at-home orders and national lockdown, it is expected that popular online streaming services such as Netflix, Twitch, Hulu, and Spotify are seeing upscale growth and development. While the amount of content creators on Twitch has slowed in the past few years, its userbase continues to rise – this is due to the various ways Twitch has included interactive media in its streaming experience, among other partnerships.

Unlike YouTube, which merely serves as a medium for all kinds of content, Twitch’s platform is integrated with each streamer’s own brand identity.

-Author

Twitch is primarily a streaming service for gaming – it is famous for its partnerships with League of Legends, Counter Strike: Global Offensive, Fortnite, and many other popular e-sports titles, each comparable (or in some cases, greater) in view count than traditional sports broadcasting events such as the NBA and MLB. By 2021, e-sports is expected to be a multi-billion dollar industry, second only to the NFL. So how has Twitch profited off of this meteoric rise in e-sports broadcasting? Not only is Twitch the prime platform to stream these largescale events (with the League of Legends World Championship matching the viewer turnout of the Superbowl), but it has developed its own interactive branded identity for each event, broadcaster, streamer, and viewer.

Central to Twitch’s brand identity is “helping you do what you do best” – this is applicable in the way they center streaming experience around the streamer, around the content creator, around “you”. Unlike YouTube, which merely serves as a medium for all kinds of content, Twitch’s platform is integrated with each streamer’s own brand identity. With its new rebranding in 2019, Twitch introduced branded assets – logos, text, and images affiliated with Twitch’s own brand identity for streamers to use in personalizing their own channel.

Additionally, Twitch also offers extensions which create an interactive experience: users can celebrate, react, share sentiment, and even vote live as they watch their favorite streamers. Moreover, these extensions allow for users to view stats in real-time on the games their streamers are currently playing, track progress on the match being played by checking inventory and gear, and even play interactive mini games on the side and in the Twitch chat log, all live in real-time. An equivalent would be if the NBA somehow allowed viewers to click on the screen to check player statistics and re-watch plays in real-time while hosting mini games on courtside with celebrities at the same time!

It is no wonder that Twitch is so successful in capturing the viewer’s attention – by having access to so much real-time information on the match at the click of a button, they may as well be playing the video game themselves. Twitch’s new logo, “you’re already one of us”, speaks to the user as streamer: the typical Twitch viewer is already integrated within the streaming channels he watches, speaking the same language through emotes and gamer jargon. Perhaps most famous for its emotes, Twitch has turned the passive side chat log into an interactive experience – not only can streamers design their own emotes which can be spammed throughout the streaming platform, it makes for a language of inside jokes and references, an interactive language that users of Twitch speak fluently. Inside or outside of Twitch, this language is able to convey meanings related to their images or can simply be the meaning themselves. Twitch has turned its very language into something interactive, something each streamer, viewer, sponsor, and user can speak with their own voice.
